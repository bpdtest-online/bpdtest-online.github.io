What is BPD?

Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) is a complex mental health condition characterized by persistent patterns of varying moods, self-image, and behavior. People with BPD often experience intense episodes of anger, depression, and anxiety that can last from a few hours to days. These emotional struggles can significantly impact relationships, self-image, and daily functioning. Learn more about BPD →

What Do Our BPD Tests Measure?

Quick Screening Test (MSI-BPD)

The McLean Screening Instrument for BPD (MSI-BPD) is a validated screening tool that assesses:

  • Emotional instability and mood swings
  • Fear of abandonment
  • Identity disturbance
  • Impulsive behaviors
  • Self-harm thoughts and behaviors
  • Chronic feelings of emptiness
  • Anger management issues
  • Stress-related paranoid thoughts

This 10-question test provides a quick assessment of BPD symptoms with high sensitivity.

Comprehensive Test (DSM-5)

Based on the diagnostic criteria from DSM-5, this test evaluates nine key areas:

  • Efforts to avoid abandonment
  • Pattern of unstable relationships
  • Unstable self-image or sense of self
  • Dangerous impulsivity (spending, sex, substance use, reckless driving, binge eating)?
  • Suicidal behavior or self-injury
  • Emotional instability and reactivity
  • Chronic feelings of emptiness
  • Inappropriate or intense anger
  • Stress-related paranoia or dissociation

This detailed assessment aligns with professional diagnostic criteria for BPD.
